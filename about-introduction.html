24 Seasons Drum

The 24 Seasons Drum is a drum performance that combines the 24 solar terms, calligraphy, and the Guangdong lion dance drum. It was co-founded in 1988 by Malaysian Chinese Chen Hui Chong and Chen Zai Fan, and in 2009, it became a national heritage project in Malaysia. The 24 Seasons Drum is an enriched and innovative form of traditional Chinese drumming art. It not only serves as a drum performance but also symbolizes the cultural heritage of the Southeast Asian Chinese community.

The performance form of the 24 Seasons Drums uses 24 single-skin large drums to represent the 24 solar terms, which are: Start of Spring, Rain Water, Awakening Insects, Spring Equinox, Pure Brightness, Grain Rain, Start of Summer, Grain Full, Grain in Ear, Summer Solstice, Slight Heat, Great Heat, Start of Autumn, Limit of Heat, White Dew, Autumn Equinox, Cold Dew, Frost's Descent, Start of Winter, Light Snow, Great Snow, Winter Solstice, Slight Cold, and Great Cold. Performers create different drum rhythms for each seasonal drum, combined with shouts, body movements, and changes in formations, forming a large-scale drum ensemble performance.
